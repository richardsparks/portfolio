National Grid

Careers website

Team lead, UX design & direction

The brief

National Grid asked us to reimagine their careers site so that it positioned them as a leading UK employer in both the New Talent and Experienced hire space.

The audience

We identifed distinct personas which included apprentices, undergraduates, careers advisors and parents. Each with their own specific needs.

Defining objectives

Balancing business goals with user needs was important to produce a great product. It became clear that our primary objective was simplification, both of the user journeys, interface and content presented.

Information architecture

Using our personas we performed a card sorting exercise to determine key pieces of information users needed and what content would entice them to apply.

Prototype

We validated the information architecture using our primary user journeys, ensuring a happy path for all of the possible scenarios.

Smart search

One feature we tested was a way for users to get to the most relevant page as quickly as possible, simplifying the user journey.

Design

We wanted to the site to feel vibrant and full of energy, we achieved this through dynamic imagery and animation.

Refining the experience

A number of iterations of the job search feature were tested to ensure the interface was as simple and intuitive to use as possible.

Results

27% relative increase in views

39% increase in time spent on the site

18% increase in return visits

70% reduction in bounce rate
