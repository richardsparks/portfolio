National Grid

Careers website

Team lead, UX design & direction

The brief

National Grid asked us to reimagine their careers site so that it positioned them as a leading UK employer in both the New Talent and Experienced hire space.

The audience

We indentifed 14 distinct personas which included apprentices, undergraduates, careers advisors & parents. Each with their own specific needs.

Defining objectives

Balancing business goals with user needs is the key to any great product. Our primary objective from this understanding was simplification.

Information Architecture

Using our personas we performed a card sorting excercise to determine the key pieces of information users needed and what content would entice them to apply.

Prototype

We validated the information architecture using our primary user journeys, ensuring a happy path for all of the possible scenarios.

Smart Search

One feature we tested was a way for users to get to the most relevant page as quickly as possible.

Design

We wanted to use the very essence of what they do, putting power into the hands of a nation, to inform the design.

Refining the experience

A number of interations of the job search feature were tested so that the interface was as simple and intuiative to use as possible.

Results

27% relative increase in views

39% increase in time spent on the site

18% increase in return visits

70% reduction in bounce rate
