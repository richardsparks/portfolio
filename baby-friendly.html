Baby Friendly

Mobile App

UX design project @ General Assembly

A life changing experience

Having a baby is a life changing experience and adapting to parenthood can bring a number of challenges.

Initial idea

A website or app to help new parents cut through the wealth of information.

Competitor analysis

There are lots of services new parents can use to good effect, it’s quite a crowded marketplace.

Affinity mapping

I interviewed four mums and one dad with ten week old babies. The main insights were that days can be repetitive and boring, but also unpredictable due to the baby’s needs. For many the lifestyle change is more of a shock than the birth.

Primary persona

Emily spends most of her time on her own at home looking after her twelve week old baby. She used to have a busy job in the city but has found being parent even more demanding.

She’s very tired due to lack of sleep and often home alone with limited adult contact. She’s quite bored as days are very repetitive and unpredictable events make planning difficult.

Her goals are to meet other parents at a similar stage and to find exciting things to do locally that she can take her baby to.

Problem

Emily has limited contact with other adults during the day and is bored and lonely.

Solution

An App that allows Emily to find things to do in the local area that are baby friendly.

Competitor design patterns

Prominent search feature, user ratings & reviews.

1st prototype

Feedback

The path was good but users didn’t fully understand what the app was for.

Solution

To introduce some text that gave the app more context.

2nd prototype

Feedback

The homepage required some thinking to use  and appeared complicated for some, it was also tricky to navigate whilst holding a baby.

Solution

Re-design the layout so that the buttons were larger and therefore easier to access with one hand.

3rd prototype

Feedback

Users weren't sure of the difference between ‘Baby Groups' and 'Events’.

Users didn’t want different sections for 'Cafes', 'Restaurants and 'Pubs'.

Buggy access and baby changing facilities were important.

Nobody used the search feature!

Final prototype

The project was well received and General Assembly used it as a case study for future iterations of the course.
